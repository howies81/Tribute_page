Here is a list of the important life events and achievements of Mother Teresa of Calcutta


  • 1910 - Born in Skopje, the current capital of the Republic of Macedonia
  • 1919 - Her father suddenly fell ill and died. Cause of death remains unknown
  • 1928 - Decided to become a nun and set off for Ireland to join the Sisters of Loreto in Dublin. Became Sister Mary Teresa after Saint Thérèse of Lisieux.
  • 1929 - Traveled on to Darjeeling, India, for the novitiate period.
  • 1931 - Made her First Profession of Vows, then sent to Calcutta to teach at Saint Mary's High School for Girls
  • 1937 - Took her Final Profession of Vows to a life of poverty, chastity and obedience and took on the title of "Mother"
  • 1944 - Became Principal of Saint Mary's High School for Girls
  • 1946 - Experienced a second calling to abandon teaching to work in the slums of Calcutta aiding the city's poorest and sickest people.
  • 1948 - Received approval to pursue new calling.
  • 1950 - Won canonical recognition for a new congregation, the Missionaries of Charity.
  • 1965 - Pope Paul VI bestowed the Decree of Praise upon the Missionaries of Charity
  • 1971 - Traveled to New York City to open her first American-based house of charity.
  • 1979 - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in recognition of her work "in bringing help to suffering humanity."
  • 1982 - Secretly went to Beirut, Lebanon, where she crossed between Christian East Beirut and Muslim West Beirut to aid children of both faiths.
  • 1982 - Returned to New York and spoke at the 40th anniversary of the United Nations General Assembly. Also opened Gift of Love, a home to care for those infected with HIV/AIDS.
  • 1995 - Publicly advocated a "no" vote in the Irish referendum to end the country's constitutional ban on divorce and remarriage
  • 1997 - Died at the age of 87
  • 2002 - First miracle recognized by Vatican
  • 2003 - Beatified as "Blessed Teresa of Calcutta" in a ceremony led by Pope John Paul II.
  • 2015 - Pope Francis issued a decree that recognized a second miracle attributed to Mother Teresa, clearing the way for her to be canonized as a saint of the Roman Catholic Church.
  • 2016 - Canonized as a saint in the Catholic Church.
